üí∞ Shipping Revenue Tracking

Track what buyers pay for shipping to get accurate profit!

  • Shipping Cost: What it costs YOU to ship (e.g., $0.74 for eBay Standard Envelope)
  • Shipping Revenue: What the BUYER pays you for shipping (e.g., $1.25-$1.74)
  • Net Shipping Profit: System adds (Revenue - Cost) to your total profit automatically
  • Auto-Fill Defaults: When you select a shipping method in Edit modal:
    • eBay Standard Envelope: Cost $0.74, Revenue $1.49 (makes you $0.75!)
    • Ground Shipping: Cost $4.99, Revenue $6.99 (makes you $2.00!)

Example: Card sells for $10, buyer pays $1.49 shipping, your cost is $0.74 ‚Üí You profit $0.75 on shipping! üéâ

üí∏ Auto-Repricing for Dead Stock

Free up cash from slow-moving inventory with smart price suggestions!

  • Smart Algorithm: System suggests price reductions based on inventory age:
    • 60-89 days old: 10% markdown suggested
    • 90-119 days old: 20% markdown suggested
    • 120+ days old: 30% markdown suggested
  • One-Click Markdown: Red "üí∏ -X%" button appears in inventory table for aging items - click to instantly apply suggested price
  • Bulk Actions: Use bulk markdown buttons above inventory table to mark down all items >90 days old at once (-10%, -20%, or -30%)
  • Confirmation Required: Bulk actions ask for confirmation before applying changes
  • Why It Works: Aging inventory = dead capital. Small price cut = faster sales = better cash flow!
  • Button Details: Hover over markdown buttons to see exact savings (e.g., "Reduce by 20% (save $4.00)")

üì• Import Sales Data from eBay & Bank Reports

Automatically sync sales data from eBay and bank reports - no manual entry!

eBay Sales Report Import

  • What it does: Matches sold eBay listings to your inventory and updates status, price, and sale date automatically
  • How to get eBay report:
    1. Go to eBay Seller Hub ‚Üí Orders ‚Üí Sold
    2. Set date range for sales you want to import
    3. Click "Download" ‚Üí Choose CSV format
    4. Make sure report includes: Item Number, Sale Date, Sale Price, Shipping
  • Matching logic: Uses eBay Item Number to find exact matches in your inventory
  • What gets updated:
    • Status ‚Üí Changed to "üí∞ Sold"
    • Sold Price ‚Üí From eBay sale price
    • Sold Date ‚Üí From eBay sale date
    • Shipping Revenue ‚Üí From shipping charged to buyer (if available)
  • Where to upload: Dashboard tab ‚Üí "Import Sales Data" section ‚Üí Click "Upload eBay Report"
  • Results: System shows how many items matched, how many couldn't be found, and lists unmatched item numbers

Bank/PayPal Report Import

  • What it does: Matches bank deposits/PayPal transactions to unsold inventory by amount and marks as sold
  • How to get bank/PayPal report:
    1. Log into your bank or PayPal account
    2. Go to transaction history or statements
    3. Export transactions as CSV
    4. Make sure report includes: Date, Amount, Description
  • Matching logic: Finds unsold items where (List Price + Shipping Revenue) matches transaction amount within $1.00
  • What gets updated:
    • Status ‚Üí Changed to "üí∞ Sold"
    • Sold Price ‚Üí From transaction amount
    • Sold Date ‚Üí From transaction date
  • Best for: Quick reconciliation when you don't have eBay item numbers handy
  • Where to upload: Dashboard tab ‚Üí "Import Sales Data" section ‚Üí Click "Upload Bank Report"

Tips for Best Results

  • Use eBay reports first: They're more accurate (match by item number) than bank reports (match by amount)
  • Keep eBay item numbers filled in: Every listed item should have its eBay item number for perfect matching
  • Review unmatched items: If items don't match, check if eBay item numbers are correct or if prices changed
  • Import regularly: Weekly imports keep your inventory current and profits accurate
  • Check the results: Import results show exactly what matched - review before closing
  • No duplicates: Already-sold items won't be updated again (safe to re-import same report)
